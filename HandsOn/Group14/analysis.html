Moby Bikes Bike Share Datasource:

Dataset Analysis:

Quantities and Values:

    HarvestTime:
  • Type: Datetime
  • Importance: High - allows data to be traced over time
  • Remarks:Range from "01/01/2022 00:00" to "31/01/2022 00:30". No missing values identified. Consider conversion to standard ISO 8601 format for better interoperability
    BikeID:
  • Type: Integer
  • Importance: High - unique identifier for bicycles.
  • Remarks:79 unique IDs ranging from 5 to 124. No null values
    Battery:
  • Type:Integer
  • Importance: High - important for tracking bike status
  • Remarks:Values between -12 and 100 (validate negative values as errors). 2793 null values require specific management or imputation
    BikeIdentifier and SpikeID:
  • Type:Integer
  • Importance: Medium - additional identifiers may be useful for different analyses or joins
  • Remarks:Unspecified range for "BikeIdentifier" and 1-120 for "SpikeID". To be explored to understand the distinctions or relationships between these IDs and "BikeID"
    BikeTypeName, EBikeProfileID, EBikeStateID:
  • Type:String, Integer
  • Importance: Medium - to be explored to understand their impact or relationship with bicycle use
  • Remarks:Limited and distinct values in each field, with no null values. Exploration needed to understand meanings and implications
    IsEBike, IsMotor, IsSmartLock:
  • Type:Boolean
  • Importance: Low/Medium - may not provide much variation or insight given the lack of diversity in the data
  • Remarks:Constant values. Check usefulness for model or future analysis
    LastGPSTime, LastRentalStart:
  • Type:Datetime
  • Importance: High - time information relevant to analysis of rental behaviour
  • Remarks:No null value. Convert to standard format if required and explore for temporal and behavioural insights
    Latitude, Longitude:
  • Type:Integer
  • Importance: Very high - essential spatial information for geographic analysis and planning
  • Remarks:Ensure data is consistent and accurate. No null values indicated.

Data Schema:

    Potential Relationships:
  • BikeID and Biketype, battery, : Understand whether there are any relationships or correspondences between these identifiers.
  • LastGPSTime, LastRentalStart, HarvestTime: Evaluate how these timestamps interact and reflect the behaviour of bikes and users.
    Potential Exploits:
  • Temporal and spatial attributes (HarvestTime, Latitude, Longitude) are essential for any temporal and geospatial analysis, such as demand prediction and route analysis.
  • Bicycle state attributes (Battery) will be central to analysing bicycle efficiency and usage.
