Naming strategy

For the naming stategy we decided to go with the base: http://www.divvybikesharing.com/group14/, and the directory where our information is at is http://www.divvybikesharing.com/group14/ontology/BikeInfo#

Divvy Dataset Datasource:

Dataset Analysis:

Quantities and Values:

    TripID:
  • Type: Integer
  • Importance: High - allows to track each trip
  • Remarks:Unique values serving as the primary key for trip records.
    BikeID:
  • Type: Integer
  • Importance: High - unique identifier for bicycles.
  • Remarks:Used to link trips to specific bicycles. No null values detected.
    StartTime and EndTime:
  • Type:Datetime
  • Importance: High - critical for determining trip durations and patterrns.
  • Remarks:Follows ISO 8601 format. Essential for temporal analysis
    TripDuration:
  • Type:Integer
  • Importance: High - important for understanding usage patterns.
  • Remarks:Values represent the duration of trips in seconds. No negative values.
    FromStationID and ToStationID:
  • Type:Integer
  • Importance: High - important for understanding usage patterns.
  • Remarks:Station IDs are critical for geographic mapping and analysis.
    FromStationName and ToStationName:
  • Type:String
  • Importance: High - important for understanding usage patterns.
  • Remarks:Values represent the locations of each station of trips.
    UserType:
  • Type:String
  • Importance: Medium - provides insight into customer segmentation
  • Remarks:typically "Customer" or "Subscriber", indicating the type of bike share user.
    Gender:
  • Type:String
  • Importance: Low/Medium - may offer demographic insights for user behavior analysis.
  • Remarks:Includes "Male", "Female", and null for non-subscribers.
    BirthYear:
  • Type:Integer
  • Importance: Medium - can be used for demographic analysis.
  • Remarks:Year of birth of the user, might contain null values for "Customer" type users.

Data Schema:

    Potential Relationships:
  • BikeID and Trips: Correlate each bike with its usage data, including trip durations and station information.
  • UserType, Gender, and BirthYear: Analyze how these user characteristics affect trip patterns and bike usage.
    Potential Exploits:
  • Temporal Data (StartTime, EndTime): Utilize for modeling peak usage times and user behavior.
  • Spatial Data (FromStationID, ToStationID): Critical for route analysis, station performance, and planning.
  • Demographics (Gender, BirthYear): Inform targeted marketing and service adjustments
